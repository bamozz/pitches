How it works

Collaborative practice, in plain English.

Most disputes do not need a courtroom. Collaborative practice is a structured way for two people, with their lawyers in the room, to work towards an agreement they can both live with. Here is what it actually looks like in Dunedin.

Talk to us about your situation
  1. 1Step

    An initial conversation

    You tell us what is going on, in your own words. We tell you whether collaborative practice or mediation is suitable, or whether something else makes more sense.

  2. 2Step

    A defined process & fee

    Before anything begins, you know what is included, what is not, and what it costs — fixed-fee options where possible.

  3. 3Step

    Working together

    Structured sessions with both parties and their lawyers, focused on agreement rather than escalation. We keep things constructive.

  4. 4Step

    A written outcome

    Once agreement is reached, we draft the formal documentation so the resolution holds up legally and practically.
